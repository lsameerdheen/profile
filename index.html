Instruments Handled

X-ray Diffraction (Bruker-D8): Used to find the phase purity, phase identification and crystallographic properties of materials.

Scanning Electron Microscopy and EDAX (JEOL, 840A): Employed to investigate the morphology and size of the powders and the impurity phases by spot analysis using EDAX.

Transmission Electron Microscopy (Tecnai-20G 2 ): Employed for microstructural analysis

High energy planetary ball mill (Fritsch): Employed for synthesis of equilibrium and non- equilibrium alloy phases

Particle size analyzer (Malvern): Used to find the particle size of the powders by laser light scattering method.

Vickers microhardness tester (Leica): Employed for microhardness measurements of individual powder particles and bulk samples.

Nanoindentation tester (MTS): Employed to extract elastic modulus and hardness of the specimen material from load-displacement measurements.

Arc Melting furnace: Used for production of metal ingots.

Thermo Gravimetric Analysis (Cahn): Used to study the stability of materials at various atmospheres.

Differential Thermal Analyzer (INS SDT-Q600): Used to study the phase decomposition/dislocation reactions and possible transformation reactions.

Differential Scanning Calorimetry (Perkin-Elmer): Used to study crystallization behaviour of materials.

Atomic absorption spectroscopy (Varian, GBC & Thermo): Utilized to find a quantity of metal ion concentration in the liquids by absorption method.

Optical Emission Spectroscopy (Varian): Used to investigate the percent of metals in the solid specimen by emission method.

Optical Microscopy: used to view the surface morphology and grain size of materials.

at Indian Institute of Technology (BHU), Varanasi and CSIR-NML, Jamshedpur, since 2011. Research Topic: Mineral matter characteristics of Indian coals Work involves evaluation of chemical and mineralogical compositions of coal samples collected from different seams of a particular borehole of Samaleswari block, Ib river coalfield, Odisha, India. Study also involves FactSage thermodynamics model to understand the ash fusion behavior to predict the phase transformations that occur during the process of coal combustion. The work has an international publication. (Note: The above work was carried in addition to doctoral study).

Research Topic: A unified approach to superplasticity in materials The objective of the work is to understand optimal structural superplasticity in micron and sub-micron grained materials and its mechanisms of deformation on the basis of a common theoretical model. The work resulted in two international publications.
